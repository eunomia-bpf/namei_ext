\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

The boundary-validity claim covers two examined upstream transitions after KVM
correctness and lower-filesystem-behavior checks pass. Operation-weighted traces and
same-oracle baselines support practical value and cost claims. Broader
family-level claims require additional classified transitions.

This artifact excludes mechanisms that require ownership beyond
object selection and visibility. Precomputed maps, FUSE, agent filesystems, and
metadata services remain valid mechanisms outside that scope. The artifact
also excludes synthetic file contents, custom metadata persistence, distributed
directory indexing, and write-conflict resolution. It also excludes data-path
mediation, storage layout, agent orchestration, cross-path transactional
snapshots, atomic multi-object commits, and consistency beyond selecting
existing lower objects.

Source systems whose code, images, evaluators, or private benchmark submodules
are unavailable can motivate workload design or provide related-work context,
but they cannot count as KVM transition evidence until their relevant oracle is
source-derived, runnable, and recorded with raw results.
