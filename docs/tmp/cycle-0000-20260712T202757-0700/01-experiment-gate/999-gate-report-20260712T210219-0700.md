## Completed Work

The literature node searched the restored story without relying on project
names or table-only diagnostics. It checked:

- `sched_ext`, eBPF LSM, VFS, fanotify, FUSE, FUSE-BPF, ExtFUSE, and RFUSE
  source families;
- Bento, Wrapfs, and stackable/custom filesystem work;
- AgentFS, BranchFS, YoloFS, Sandlock, Mirage, and related agent/workspace
  source systems;
- SWE-Factory, MEnvAgent/MEnvData-SWE, SWE-rebench V2, and DockSmith as
  environment/cache source families.

The node found medium same-claim risk. Nearby work is real and reviewer-
relevant, but the checked source families do not appear to make the same
combined claim: a `sched_ext`-style VFS name-resolution extension where a
bounded eBPF policy chooses lookup/readdir path-view behavior while the kernel
and lower filesystem retain VFS object and ordinary data semantics.

## Gate Decision

Accepted for BOOTSTRAP writing:

- The paper should carry the ambitious story that `namei_ext` evaluates a
  missing middle point between eBPF LSM access-control hooks and FUSE/custom
  filesystem ownership.
- RQ1 remains expressiveness/sufficiency of a narrow VFS name-resolution
  extension under real source-derived oracles.
- RQ2 remains cost/overhead versus feature-equivalent FUSE under the same
  oracle.
- RQ3 remains safety/ownership-boundary comparison versus custom or stackable
  filesystem ownership.
- Materialized namespace mechanisms are background or source behavior, not the
  main experimental opponent.
- Table-only and static-table impossibility remain retired as novelty lines.

Rejected or deferred:

- Do not claim exclusive necessity for `namei_ext` or eBPF.
- Do not make a generic "FUSE is slow" claim without same-oracle measurement.
- Do not claim full reproduction for source systems whose public code, images,
  private benchmark harnesses, or live service backends were not run.
- Do not include service/config as a weak breadth row; admit it only after a
  concrete lookup-time source oracle is selected.
