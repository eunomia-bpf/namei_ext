## Accepted Candidate Contract

The candidate paper story remains:

- `namei_ext` is a `sched_ext`-style VFS name-resolution extension point.
- A bounded eBPF policy decides lookup and directory-enumeration actions.
- The kernel and lower filesystem keep ownership of VFS objects, permissions,
  file methods, data path, page cache, writes, persistence, and ordinary
  consistency.
- The paper positions this boundary between eBPF LSM and FUSE/custom
  filesystem ownership.

Accepted RQs:

- RQ1: Can a narrow VFS name-resolution extension express real
  state-dependent path-view policies without taking over filesystem semantics?
- RQ2: What is the cost of putting programmable policy on the VFS
  name-resolution path compared with a feature-equivalent FUSE policy?
- RQ3: Does `namei_ext` provide a narrower and safer implementation boundary
  than custom or stackable filesystem ownership when the needed policy is only
  name resolution?

Accepted experiment shape:

- Experiment A: Agent workspace lifecycle, headline workload.
- Experiment B: Environment/cache transition, decisive non-agent workload.
- Experiment C: Service/config transition, conditional only.

Accepted comparison discipline:

- FUSE is the central RQ2 baseline and must be feature-equivalent.
- Source/native behavior is the correctness oracle, not a baseline win
  condition.
- Custom/stackable/source-system ownership is the RQ3 boundary evidence.
- Lower-FS/no-hook runs are controls.
- bind/Overlay/projected/copy/symlink/table mechanisms are related-work or
  source-specific context unless a final admitted oracle makes one directly
  load-bearing.
